I am a Post-Doctoral Researcher at the University of Florida working in the areas related to IoT security and privacy with Dr. Daniela Oliveira. My research spans across the areas of security and privacy in/using computing devices, machine learning, the human aspect of cybersecurity, human-computer interaction, and mobile and wearable sensible user behavior. My dissertation explored new authentication/deauthentication and privacy paradigms, from both offensive and defensive perspectives, in/using mobile, wearable, and IoT computing leveraging various embedded sensors (e.g., audio sensors, such as microphones and speakers, and motion-position sensors, such as accelerometer and gyroscope). My research articles have been published at various top security venues, including CCS, NDSS, WiSec, and ACM-CSUR and received considerable attention at major news outlets, such as Forensic Magazine, Science Daily, IT Security News, TechXplore, Naked Security, Webroot, Scientific Computing, Helpnet Security, SD Times, ECN, etc. I obtained my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Alabama at Birmingham (UAB), USA, and a Bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering from Tribhuvan University, Nepal.
